Logo System

Your logo comes in 9 variations across three formats and three color treatments. Here are the three you will use most often.

Full Color Primary

Your go-to logo. Use on white or light backgrounds -- websites, business cards, flyers, letterhead, invoices.

White Icon

For dark or blue backgrounds. Social media profile pictures, favicons, app icons, branded merchandise.

Black Horizontal

For single-color printing -- faxes, stamps, one-color print runs, or when color printing is not available.

Minimum Clear Space

1x 1x 1x 1x

Maintain clear space equal to at least the height of the shield on all four sides. No text, graphics, or other logos should enter this zone.

Usage Rules

Follow these rules to maintain brand integrity across all materials -- print, digital, social, and merchandise.

✓ Do

  • Always maintain clear space around the logo
  • Use the white logo on dark backgrounds, full color on light backgrounds
  • Keep the shield proportions -- never stretch or distort
  • Use brand colors consistently across all materials
  • Use Shield Blue as the dominant color in any layout (60%)
  • Reserve red for emergency and CPR-specific content only

✗ Don't

  • Don't change logo colors or add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
  • Don't place the full color logo on busy or photo backgrounds
  • Don't rotate, crop, or rearrange logo elements
  • Don't use red as a background color -- it is too aggressive
  • Don't mix Montserrat or Inter with other typefaces
  • Don't use the logo smaller than 32px wide (digital) or 0.5" (print)
