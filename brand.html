Typography

ReadyNest uses a two-tier type system: licensed print fonts for premium materials, and web-safe alternatives for digital applications.

Headlines
Safety Starts Here
Print: Club Type Mercurius Medium -- 80pt for primary headers
Web: Montserrat 700 (Bold) -- used as the web-safe alternative
Subheaders
Certified CPR Training for Childcare Professionals
Print: Circular Pro Black -- 40pt for subheaders
Web: Montserrat 600 (Semi-Bold) -- used as the web-safe alternative
Body Text
ReadyNest Safety Training delivers AHA-certified CPR courses, continuing education credits, and emergency preparedness plans built specifically for childcare centers across the greater Houston area.
Print: Circular Pro Book -- 20pt for body copy
Web: Inter 400 (Regular) -- used as the web-safe alternative
Licensing note: Club Type Mercurius and Circular Pro are licensed typefaces. They should be used in print materials, presentations, and any context where font embedding is controlled. For web, email, and digital platforms where font licensing is not guaranteed, use Montserrat (headlines/subheads) and Inter (body) as approved alternatives.

Minimum Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to at least the height of the shield on all four sides. No text, graphics, or other logos should enter this zone.

Usage Rules

Follow these rules to maintain brand integrity across all materials -- print, digital, social, and merchandise.

✓ Do

  • Always maintain clear space around the logo (at least the height of the shield on all sides)
  • Use the white logo on dark backgrounds, full color on light backgrounds
  • Keep the shield proportions -- never stretch or distort
  • Use brand colors consistently across all materials
  • Use Shield Blue as the dominant color in any layout (target 60%)
  • Reserve red tones for emergency and CPR-specific content only

✗ Don't

  • Don't change logo colors or add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
  • Don't place the full color logo on busy or photographic backgrounds
  • Don't rotate, crop, or rearrange logo elements
  • Don't use Emergency Red as a background color -- it is too aggressive for surfaces
  • Don't mix brand fonts with other typefaces in the same material
  • Don't use Bright Red as a dominant color -- limit to CTA buttons and urgent callouts
